First step
  • Gather only the two ingredients that are needed (peanuts without shell and icing sugar)
    • Second step
  • Toast the natural peanuts in a pan over low heat. Wait until they look toasted and remove from heat. Why toast them? Because its flavor is enhanced.

    Tip: Toast the peanuts but do not burn them, but their flavor will be bitter.
    • Third step
  • Crush the peanuts with the help of a mortar, until turning them into powder or you can also liquefy them. In my case, I first grind them with a mortar and then I passed them through a manual blender. Why such a long process? To avoid forcing the motor of the blender. The final consistency of the peanut marzipan must be homogeneous, without lumps and without solid peanut remains. Note that as the peanuts are crushed, they release a natural oil that facilitates mixing.
    • Fourth step
  • Add the icing sugar little by little (snow sugar) to the ground peanut. Stop from time to time to peel off the content that sticks to the walls of the glass with the help of a wooden spoon. The final consistency of ground peanut butter will be a smooth but firm, manageable and smooth dough.

    Tip: You must get a homogeneous mass and if it is necessary to pass the mixture several times through the blender, do it.
    • Fifth step
  • Using the hands knead the peanut paste until it is compact and homogeneous.

    Tip: You can flour a flat surface slightly and spread on it the dough you have made. The flour can be cornstarch or the same snow.
    • Sixth step
  • Spread the dough on the table and shape the marzipan peanut candy with the help of a cookie cutter or a cookie cutter. Place the marzipan on a tray with parchment paper. Once ready, you should let them stand for at least 1 hour in the fridge, so the dough will harden slightly.
